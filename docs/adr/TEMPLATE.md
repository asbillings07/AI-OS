## Context

What situation forces a decision? What's the problem, the constraints, the forces in tension? State the facts and the pressures — not the answer yet. Enough that a reader years from now understands *why this even came up*.

## Why now?

Why is this decision being made *now* rather than later? What does deciding unblock, or what does *not* deciding cost? (Borrowed from Rust's RFC process — this prevents speculative ADRs. If you can't answer "why now," it may belong in an issue or design doc instead.)

## Decision

The decision, stated plainly and in the active voice: **"We will …"** Be specific enough to be actionable and to be checked against.

## In one sentence

> A single distilled takeaway readers can quote and reference years later. (People skim ADRs — give them the one line that matters.)
